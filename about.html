My ideals

Find out more about me

As a person:

I believe that patients are entitled to three things, absolute honesty, my best care possible, and a healthier smile/life. I never want to lose sight of the person in the chair, and believe that patients should feel totally comfortable and confident. I truly believe that having a fantastic patient experience goes hand in hand with having successful clinical outcomes.

As a practitioner:

A great dentist is one who gives confidence to their patients. One who is objective and honest, without making assumptions. One who has the ability to tailor the type of care and patient experience to what the individual wants and needs. A great dentist is one who can clearly communicate what is ailing the patient and provide the appropriate amount of influence to help patients understand and want treatment. Creating buy-in and ownership on the patient’s end takes a true understanding of what is and has happened in the mouth; this is only possible through intentional collaboration and clear communication.

The responsibility between patient and doctor are equal and even. Each needs to have respect for and listen to the other, because there is much to be learned from both. Each needs to have confidence in the other, and speak with honesty and forthrightness.

My aim for my team is to be as clear as possible. Communication is the foundation of leadership. If a team member makes a mistake, it’s probably because I have failed somewhere along the line of communication or expectations. The work environment will be one where it’s ok to make mistakes, with the expectation of learning and getting better at what we do everyday. Our team culture will be based on trust, growth, and communication.

My Mission:

It’s my responsibility to improve the oral health of the community which I'm in. In order to do that, patients need trust, competence, and genuine caring for the human that is attached to the mouth. It is my goal for patients to love the way they are treated so much that they refer their friends and family. It’s my obligation as a provider to ensure that patients feel safe, heard, and cared for. When done ethically, profitability is a direct measurement of impact in the community. I am very interested in practice systems and marketing optimization to try to create as much positive community impact as possible.

My responsibility to dentistry is a perspective shift: of how patients view their oral health, how patients view dentistry, and how different the standard of care can be. I will care for the person first, while I treat what ails them.
